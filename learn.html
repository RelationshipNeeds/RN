The Essential Relationship Resource for the College Experience

Why students need relationship skills to thrive

College is a time of transition—students are navigating independence, relationships, and personal growth. Strong relationship skills not only support mental and emotional well-being but also enhance academic performance, teamwork, and resilience during these formative years.

The only book that defines—and solves—our core relationship Needs

Every student hears that communication and connection are keys to healthy relationships, but few understand the why behind conflict or disconnection. Relationship Needs is the first and only book that pinpoints our core Behavioral Needs and provides 10 proven shortcuts and 188 tips to fulfill them in real time.

A practical resource for classes, counseling, and student life

From psychology and behavioral science courses to peer-counseling programs and resident life initiatives, Relationship Needs gives students a shared language for understanding themselves and others. With simple, science-backed guidance, it helps them reduce stress, navigate conflict, and form lasting, meaningful connections.

Perfect for psychology, sociology, and honors seminars

With its blend of empirical behavioral research and accessible storytelling, Relationship Needs is ideal as a supplemental text for psychology or sociology classes. It also works well in honors seminars, where students are encouraged to explore the intersection of personal development, emotional intelligence, and social dynamics.
